Fast Fashion

For this project I researched the impact of fast fashion on people and the environment. The central question was: what does cheap clothing actually cost? From water usage and CO2 emissions to working conditions and textile waste, I translated complex data into an interactive multimedia story built in Figma.

To push myself further I also experimented with AI tools to generate video content. It was a way for me to explore new techniques while making something that actually gets people to think twice before buying their next cheap outfit.

The project was aimed at young people and social media users who shop online a lot.

process

Research

I started by collecting data on water usage, CO2, working conditions, textile waste and the situation in the Netherlands. This became the foundation of the whole story.

Storymap V1

The first version was a straight linear flow, step by step from ordering a shirt to where it ends up. Clean and simple, but not very engaging yet.

Feedback Meeting

After a meeting it became clear the story needed more interaction. Videos, scroll sections and moments where the user can choose what they want to explore.

Storymap V2

The second version included video with voice-over, an interactive map and scrollable data sections. A much richer experience than the first version.

Prototype in Figma

The final result is a working prototype in Figma. I also used AI to generate video content as a new technique I picked up during this project.
